# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the Altair
community a harassment-free experience for everyone.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
and healthy community.

**Special Commitment to Neurodiversity:**

Altair is built by and for the neurodivergent community, with a particular focus
on ADHD. We are committed to creating a space that:

- Recognizes neurodiversity as natural human variation, not deficit
- Accommodates different communication styles and needs
- Practices patience with executive function challenges
- Celebrates diverse ways of thinking and problem-solving
- Provides clear structure while allowing for flexibility

## Our Standards

### Examples of Behavior That Contributes to a Positive Environment

**General Conduct:**

- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members

**Neurodiversity-Affirming Behaviors:**

- Understanding that ADHD brains work differently
- Being patient with communication differences
- Offering reminders without judgment
- Celebrating varying approaches to problems
- Accommodating different processing speeds
- Respecting hyperfocus and need for breaks
- Using clear, concrete language
- Providing structure and clear expectations

**Collaboration:**

- Giving credit where it's due
- Welcoming newcomers
- Mentoring less experienced contributors
- Sharing knowledge generously
- Asking for help when needed
- Respecting boundaries and capacity limits

### Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

**Harassment:**

- The use of sexualized language or imagery
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments
- Personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate

**Neurodivergence:**

- Dismissing neurodivergent experiences
- Shaming for ADHD symptoms (forgetfulness, lateness, etc.)
- Assuming incompetence based on disability
- Refusing reasonable accommodations
- Making fun of accessibility needs
- Using "ADHD" as an insult or joke
- Telling people to "just focus" or "try harder"

**Exclusionary Behavior:**

- Deliberate intimidation or stalking
- Sustained disruption of discussions
- Unwelcome following or photography
- Pattern of inappropriate social contact
- Continued one-on-one communication after requests to stop

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of
acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in
response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive,
or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not
aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation
decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Discord server (when launched)
- Social media channels
- Email communications
- Community events (virtual or in-person)
- Any other official Altair community space

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing
the community in public spaces. Examples include using an official email address,
posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed
representative at an online or offline event.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at:

**Primary Contact:** <hello@getaltair.app>

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

When reporting, please include:

- Your contact information
- Names (usernames, real names) of individuals involved
- Description of the incident
- Any relevant context
- Links or screenshots if applicable
- Whether you've talked to the person involved
- Any other information that may be helpful

**Confidentiality:**
All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the
reporter of any incident. We will not share reporter information without explicit
permission, except as required by law.

### What Happens After a Report

1. **Acknowledgment:** We will acknowledge receipt of your report within 24 hours
2. **Investigation:** We will review the incident and gather additional information
   if needed
3. **Decision:** We will determine appropriate consequences based on the severity
4. **Communication:** We will communicate the outcome to the reporter
5. **Action:** We will take the determined action
6. **Follow-up:** We will check in with the reporter after resolution

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining
the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed
unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from community leaders, providing
clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the
behavior was inappropriate.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No
interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with
those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This
includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels
like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public
communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or
private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction
with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period.
Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community
standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an
individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Appeals

If you believe you have been unfairly accused of violating this Code of Conduct,
you may appeal the decision by contacting <hello@getaltair.app> with a concise
description of your grievance. Appeals will be reviewed by community leaders who
were not involved in the original decision.

## Accommodations

We recognize that neurodivergent individuals may sometimes struggle with social
norms or communication. When possible, we will:

1. **Assume Good Faith:** First assume misunderstanding rather than malice
2. **Provide Clear Feedback:** Explain what behavior was problematic and why
3. **Offer Education:** Share resources about inclusive communication
4. **Allow Growth:** Give people opportunity to learn and improve
5. **Consider Context:** Take into account disability-related factors

However, impact matters more than intent. Harm caused, even unintentionally,
still requires accountability and change.

## ADHD-Specific Guidelines

### For Contributors

**If you have ADHD and are contributing:**

- It's okay to ask for clarification multiple times
- You can request deadline extensions if needed
- You may hand off issues if overwhelmed
- Your contribution pace is your own
- Breaks are encouraged and expected
- Incomplete work is still valuable

**If you're interacting with ADHD contributors:**

- Provide clear, written instructions
- Break down requests into steps
- Be patient with questions
- Offer structure without rigidity
- Celebrate small wins
- Don't shame for ADHD symptoms

### Common ADHD-Related Scenarios

**Scenario:** Someone misses deadlines or forgets commitments
**Response:** Gentle reminder, offer tools/support, no shame

**Scenario:** Someone asks the same question multiple times
**Response:** Patient re-explanation, consider documentation improvement

**Scenario:** Someone hyperfocuses and over-contributes feedback
**Response:** Appreciate enthusiasm, gently guide toward focused input

**Scenario:** Someone's communication seems blunt or direct
**Response:** Don't assume rudeness, ask for clarification if unsure
